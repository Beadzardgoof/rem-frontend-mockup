Our Story

Over a century of art in the heart of Lafayette.

Founded in 1909

Established on April 14, 1909 as the Lafayette Art Association, the museum was created to stimulate interest in art, hold annual exhibitions, and build a permanent collection for the community. Over a century later, that founding purpose still guides everything we do.

Our Collection

The museum's permanent collection features over 1,400 works of primarily American art — from regional painters to nationally recognized artists. Rotating exhibitions bring fresh perspectives to our galleries every 3–6 months, so there is always something new to discover.

Accreditation & Community

In 1982 the museum earned accreditation from the American Alliance of Museums — a distinction held by fewer than 5% of museums nationally. We are the only accredited art museum in Northwest Indiana, and we remain committed to keeping our doors open and our admission free.
